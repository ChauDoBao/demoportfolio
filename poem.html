I AM (NOT) OKAY NOW

Coder

“I am (not) okay now” is an interactive web-based poem designed to convey the emotional turmoil and lingering feelings one experiences after the end of a significant relationship. It explores the deep pain of loss, self-reflection, and the struggle to move forward. As the name suggests, the poetic main character believed they had reached the stage of acceptance, only to realize they had not.

This poem, inspired by Baekhyun's "Cry for Love," explores love, loss, guilt, and the struggle of letting go. Co-written with ChatGPT for rhythmic phrasing, it uses vivid imagery to depict haunting memories of a once joyful love now turned to sorrow. Unlike breakups filled with bitterness, it portrays a beautiful relationship that simply ends. The visuals evoke a subtle melancholy, mirroring the protagonist's hidden emotions.

This is my first attempt on coding, using fundamental HTML, CSS and Javascript. At that stage I did not made it responsive so it is recommended to view in 1440px screen size.

THE POEM

I once was wrapped in love so deep,
A promise made I couldn’t keep.
I swore I'd never fall again,
A vow, a shield, a heart to mend.

I sift through tokens of our past,
Brief glances, then they’re gone, cast.
A wish for you, a hope to rise—
Yet why this tug, this lurking lie?

The silent thoughts, they haunt my sleep,
A question cuts, a wound too steep.
Even when the mirror shows new hair,
Or melodies I find elsewhere.

I know the fault, I own the blame,
This time, this time, I bear the shame.
I try to soothe this aching chest,
Why is it so hard to rest?

Each corner holds a shape of you,
Each echo says we’re split in two.
I leave behind these haunted halls,
Where shadows write on empty walls.

Yet still, I search as if by chance,
A glimpse of you, a fleeting glance.
Lost in spaces, lost in time,
What once was ours, no longer mine.

You were my moon, my stars, my light,
Now clouds obscure the endless night.
Your voice once cut through every din,
Now silence lingers deep within.

Rain pours down to hide my tears,
Mascara streaks with unvoiced fears.
You were my spring, my bloom, my song,
Now winter’s where I linger long.
